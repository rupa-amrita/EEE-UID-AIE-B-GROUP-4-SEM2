RESEARCH GAP


  1. Energy Efficiency Optimization Gap: Most smart street lighting systems use traditional motion or ambient light sensors. There is limited research on AI-driven adaptive lighting that optimizes energy use based on real-time environmental and traffic conditions.

  2. Integration with Renewable Energy: Many existing studies focus on IoT-based lighting control but do not explore the integration of solar or wind energy with AI for energy-efficient operation.

  3. Predictive Maintenance and Fault Detection: AI-powered predictive maintenance can prevent failures, but research on real-time fault detection and self-healing networks in smart lighting is still limited.

  4. Security and Privacy Concerns: IoT devices in smart lighting are open to cyberattacks. There is a lack of strong security mechanisms like AI-powered un-even pattern detection to prevent hacking an

  5. Traffic and Pedestrian Behavior Analysis: Existing systems mainly use motion sensors, but few studies explore AI-based traffic and pedestrian analytics to adjust lighting dynamically for safety and efficiency.

  6. Scalability and Cost-Effectiveness: Research is needed on how to make smart street lighting more affordable for large-scale deployment, particularly in developing countries.
