Problem Statement

  1. Implementation Barriers & costs: Upgrading from traditional to automated street lighting requires significant investment in hardware,software and connectivity infrastructure.

  2. Sensor-based systems: if poorly calibrated lead to frequent malfunction and high replacement costs.

  3. Sensor Reliability Issues:Environmental factors such as dust, rain, fog, and extreme temperatures can affect the accuracy and durability of light and motion sensors.

  4. Communication Failures:In wireless-controlled lighting systems, connectivity issues can cause failures in transmitting control signals.

  5. In modern urban environments, traditional street lighting systems suffer from inefficient energy consumption, high maintenance costs, and lack of real-time adaptability. While IoT-based smart lighting solutions have emerged, they often rely on fixed schedules, basic motion sensors, or manual control, leading to suboptimal energy utilization and operational inefficiencies.

  6. Furthermore, existing smart street lighting systems lack AI-driven predictive analytics for dynamic lighting control, predictive maintenance, and real-time fault detection.

  7. This research aims to address these gaps by developing an AI and IoT-enabled smart street lighting system that enhances energy efficiency, predictive maintenance, security, and real-time adaptability, contributing to sustainable and intelligent urban infrastructure.

  8. In wireless-controlled lighting systems, connectivity issues can cause failures in transmitting control signals. So for this we ensure any such mistakes will not happen.
